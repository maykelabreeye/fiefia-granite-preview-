How We Work

Four steps, start to finish.

01 — Consultation

Walk your space

We visit your home or site, listen to your vision, and advise on the right material for your lifestyle, budget, and light.

02 — Selection

Pick your exact slab

Meet us at the supplier yard. You choose the slab you want, marked on the spot — no surprises on delivery day.

Materials

Every stone has a story.

We work with four primary materials — each with its own personality, care profile, and best use. Here's a quick field guide to help you decide.

Igneous · Natural

Granite

Formed over millions of years under extreme heat and pressure, granite is one of the hardest natural surfaces available. Each slab is one of a kind — flecked, veined, or pooled with minerals in patterns no lab can reproduce.

Hardness
Very High

Excellent
Care

Metamorphic · Natural

Marble

The classical choice for refined interiors. Marble is softer than granite and more porous, which makes it a poor match for heavy-use prep zones — but its veining and luminosity are unmatched for islands, vanities, and statement walls.

Hardness
Medium
Heat
Good
Care
Seal Regularly
Best For
Baths · Baking
Engineered · Non-Porous

Quartz

Ninety-plus percent natural quartz crystals bound with resin. Consistent, stain-resistant, and never needs sealing. The right choice for busy families and commercial installs where predictability matters.

Soapstone

Quiet, architectural, and naturally non-porous. Soapstone darkens and develops a soft patina with use — a living surface that rewards the cooks who pick it. No sealing required, just an occasional wipe of mineral oil.
